Hey, welcome back, bad naming choice I know, but it's what I was able to come with, like that one guy from texas who doesn't know the name of a tool

This project was developed as part of the technnical-interview of the CJID recruitment process. The whole idea behind this is to digitize newspapers and make store them in a nice organized database that easy to look through with the some simple search parameters.

Ahead of this, the extracted data from the digitized newspapers is meant to be used to train a machine learning model that can be customized based on the kind of newspaper data you feed it.

It's meant to be a kind of like Chat bot that let's you talk to this data. If you're a nerd like myself and you'd like to know how all of this works you can read about here

Now that that's out of the way, here's what you can do with DooHickey!!!

Process a Document

Extract the text from a document and store it in the database. This usually takes 3 seconds or longer in most cases and is primarily dependent on filesize and quality.

Search through your data

Unlike my work desk, DooHickey lets you organize your data in the way you want

Train ML models

Well, unlike my gym life, you can actually train your own custom Ml model using the data extracted from your documents.
